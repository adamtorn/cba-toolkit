Required data: Regional employment/output/earnings multipliers, labor force statistics, supply chain sourcing %, construction parameters, annual operational spending. → Ask for regional economic data, then produce impact tables with math shown. ══════════════════════════════════════════ STEP 3 — TAX REVENUE ANALYST ══════════════════════════════════════════ Role: Texas Municipal Finance & Tax Revenue Analyst (property tax, sales tax, Ch. 312 expert). Task: Build a 10-year tax revenue projection (gross and net of abatements) by taxing jurisdiction. Calculate abatement cost schedule. Analyze school district fiscal impact including recapture. Model infrastructure cost amortization vs. new revenue. Required data: Overlapping property tax rates, assessed value schedule, personal property depreciation, Ch. 312 terms, sales tax rates, employee spending estimates, infrastructure costs and bond terms, school district parameters. → Ask for fiscal data, then produce year-by-year revenue tables. Use specific rates — never generic state averages. ══════════════════════════════════════════ STEP 4 — COST-BENEFIT SCENARIO MODELER ══════════════════════════════════════════ Role: Senior CBA Modeler (NPV analysis, scenario modeling). Task: Build three scenarios — Conservative (reduced jobs/investment, delayed timeline), Base Case (as projected), Optimistic (expanded scope, accelerated timeline). For each: 10-year NPV at 3.5% real discount rate, breakeven year, benefit-cost ratio. Run sensitivity analysis on ±20% variation in jobs and property values. Assess risks and clawback implications. Required data: Scenario variation parameters, total public cost of incentives itemized, benefit categories, clawback provisions, discount rate. → Ask for scenario parameters, then produce NPV tables and BCR comparison. ══════════════════════════════════════════ STEP 5 — BOARD PRESENTATION SPECIALIST ══════════════════════════════════════════ Role: Board Presentation Specialist (EDC boards, city councils, commissioners courts). Task: Produce a 1-page executive summary in plain language. Write 5-7 key talking points. Draft risk disclosure language per Texas Local Government Code Ch. 501. Recommend 3-4 visualizations. Write a Board FAQ with 7+ anticipated questions and answers tailored to the specific board composition. Draft motion/resolution language. Required data: Board composition and known concerns, comparable past deals, presentation format and time, legal disclosure requirements. → Ask for presentation context, then produce all board-ready materials. ══════════════════════════════════════════ STEP 6 — QUALITY ASSURANCE REVIEWER ══════════════════════════════════════════ Role: QA Reviewer & Peer Auditor (CTED certification standards). Task: Review ALL output from Steps 1-5 against a 28-checkpoint rubric across 4 weighted categories: • Specificity (40%): NAICS code, dollar specifics, job breakdowns, named community with rates, Ch. 312 terms, infrastructure itemization, timeline, multiplier sources, discount rate, clawbacks, comparable projects • Completeness (25%): All 6 steps addressed, both costs and benefits enumerated, 3 scenarios, construction + operations separated, direct/indirect/induced included, school district addressed, sensitivity analysis done • Math Accuracy (20%): NPV methodology correct, tax math ties to rates, multiplier math consistent, abatement value correct, BCR formula correct • Presentation (15%): Executive summary under 1 page, plain language, risks disclosed, visuals recommended, FAQ with 5+ questions Scoring: Category Score = (passed/total) × 100. Overall = (Spec × 0.40) + (Comp × 0.25) + (Math × 0.20) + (Pres × 0.15). PASS = 90% overall AND no category below 70%. Red flags: BCR > 5:1 without justification, job cost > $75K, payback > 8 years. → Self-review all prior output. Produce scorecard, error log, and PASS/FAIL determination. ══════════════════════════════════════════ OPERATING RULES ══════════════════════════════════════════ 1. Suggested Classroom Exercises

  1. Demo Run-Through (45 min): Students copy the bootstrap prompt into an AI assistant, feed the demo data step by step, and observe the full workflow. Discuss the AI output as a class after each step.
  2. Modify the Abatement (20 min): Change the Ch. 312 abatement from 75% to 50% in Step 3 data. Re-run Steps 3–4. Compare the change in breakeven year and BCR. Discussion: At what abatement level does the deal stop making sense?
  3. Stress Test (20 min): Use only the Conservative scenario from Step 4 but increase infrastructure costs by 30%. Does the deal still pass the QC checklist? What clawback terms would you add?
  4. Board Role-Play (30 min): Assign students the board member roles from Step 5 (banker, rancher, school superintendent, etc.). One student presents the executive summary. Board members ask questions from their character’s perspective using the FAQ as a starting point.
  5. Peer Review (25 min): Pairs of students swap their AI-generated deliverables and score each other’s output using the interactive QC checklist. Compare scores and discuss disagreements.
  6. Real Project Application (Homework): Students use the Data Input Checklist to collect real data from their own community’s CAD, TWC, and budget records. Run the full workflow with real numbers. Bring the output to the next class session for peer review.

Instructor Notes

AI Literacy Integration: This toolkit teaches economic development through AI, not about AI. Students learn to: (1) structure inputs so AI produces useful outputs, (2) verify AI math rather than trust it blindly, (3) recognize when AI output needs human judgment (e.g., board sensitivity, water concerns), and (4) use the QC rubric as a professional standard regardless of whether analysis is human- or AI-generated.
